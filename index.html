About

Chris Delp is a highly regarded Systems Engineer specializing in Model-Based Systems Engineering (MBSE). Throughout his career, he has been instrumental in advancing the discipline of systems engineering for complex space missions and actively contributes to the global standards and educational programs that shape the future of the field.

Professional Affiliations & Contributions

  • NASA Jet Propulsion Laboratory (JPL): Chris plays a critical role at NASA JPL, applying advanced systems engineering and MBSE methodologies to deep space exploration missions. His work helps manage the immense complexity of robotic spacecraft, ensuring mission success through rigorous modeling and simulation.
  • Caltech CTME (Center for Technology and Management Education): As an instructor and subject matter expert at Caltech CTME, Chris helps educate the next generation of engineers and industry professionals. He teaches professional development programs focused on Systems Engineering, Systems Architecture, and MBSE, bridging the gap between theoretical models and practical, aerospace-grade application.
  • INCOSE (International Council on Systems Engineering): An active contributor to INCOSE, Chris helps drive the global dialogue on systems engineering best practices. He is heavily involved in the MBSE initiative, helping organizations transition from document-centric to model-centric engineering.
  • OMG (Object Management Group): Chris has been a pivotal figure in the development and standardization of systems modeling languages. Through OMG, he has contributed to the evolution of SysML (Systems Modeling Language), providing the technical leadership necessary to ensure the language meets the rigorous demands of real-world aerospace and defense applications.
